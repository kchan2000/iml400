On cybernetic gardens

Readings:
The Editors of Logic Mag, Where Deer Stroll Peacefully Past Computers
Cybernetics Conference Case Study

“Where Deer Stroll Peacefully Past Computers” dug deeper into a perspective of technology that I hadn’t thought too deeply about before, and that is: how care plays into technology, or rather how there isn’t enough care in tech currently. I loved how recent and relevant these examples of care and tech were because I believe everyone’s relationship with technology changed during the pandemic. For myself, I see a much heavier reliance on tech in different ways (Zoom for everything, 2-hour long Facetimes with friends I haven’t seen for almost a year, and even stalking the Spotify Friend’s Feed to try to feel some sort of collective presence). In this regard, I feel grateful for technology in keeping society moving forward in some ways. But at the same time, I see my screen time skyrocketing (consuming upwards of almost a quarter of the hours in my day), alongside higher feelings of anxiety I didn’t feel before. This is what made what they were exploring, “what a more habitable digital world might look like,” all the more interesting. Using the word “habitable” to describe what we don’t have was accurate. There is not a sustainable healthy way we interact with technology...yet.

Does cutting out tech and social media have to mean social isolation?

How can we stay connected while also reducing screen time?

What has the pandemic taught us about our relationship with technology and how we can improve it?
